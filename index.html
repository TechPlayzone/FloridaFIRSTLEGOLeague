What is FLL?

FIRST® LEGO® League (FLL) introduces STEM (science, technology, engineering, and math) to children ages 9-14 through fun, exciting hands-on learning. Participants gain real-world problem-solving experiences through a guided, global robotics program using LEGO® technology.
The FLL competition evaluates teams in four key areas: Core Values, Innovation Project, Robot Design, and Robot Performance.These areas are weighted equally during the tournament which helps determine the overall ranking of the teams.

UNEARTHED Theme

The UNEARTHED theme for the 2025-2026 season of FIRST® LEGO® League invites teams to explore the past, uncover hidden stories, and use STEM to envision a brighter future. Teams will build and code autonomous LEGO Education robots to complete a series of archaeology-themed missions, simulating real-world challenges faced by archaeologists. Additionally, teams invent solutions to real-world problems faced by archeologists. The theme encourages teams to think critically about how the past can inform our future and to develop innovative solutions for the future.

Pit Area and Tailgating Area

The Pit is your team's home base when working on the robot and coding during the competition. The Pit is for coaches and team members only. The Pit is not the space for teams to congregate all day long. The Tailgating Area is for teams to have down time during the tournament. Parents and guaardians can set up tents, tables, and chairs outdoors. Create interactive displays and encourage teams to engage at your tailgating area.

Pit Area

The Pit Area will have charging stations. There is no need for teams to bring extension cords or surge protections. There will also be an area for Practice Tables. Teams are not allowed to bring Field Kits, Mission Mats or Models in the Pit. Each Pit space will have:

  • One 6ft table
  • At least 2 chairs
  • Robot and supplies
  • Laptop and power cord
  • USB download cable (no Bluetooth!)
  • Robot transport container

Volunteer Requirements

Help make this championship possible!

MANDATORY TEAM REQUIREMENT

Every team must provide at least one adult volunteer (18+) to serve as the team's Queuer. Teams that don't meet this requirement will not receive their registration packet and schedule.

Queuer Responsibilities

Queuers manage team traffic and maintain event pace:

  • For Judging: Ensure team arrives 5 minutes early and enters room on time
  • For Robot Game: Get team to competition area 5 minutes before match
  • Track multiple schedules simultaneously
  • Line up team in proper tournament table lane

Mandatory Meeting

Saturday, February 21: 7:45 AM

Location: Volunteer Room

ALL queuers must attend this meeting. If you're a coach serving as your team's queuer, notify organizers immediately and still sign up at www.firstinspires.org

Benefits

  • Work alongside your team
  • High visibility role
  • Contribute to event success
  • Observe matches
  • Build leadership skills

Physically demanding: Must be able to stand for extended periods
